<b>Abstract</b>
The Indian legal system relies heavily on complex, dense English terminology (legalese)
that acts as a significant barrier for the common citizen. In regions like Maharashtra,
individuals often sign rent agreements, employment contracts, and loan documents
without fully understanding the underlying terms due to a lack of vernacular accessibility
and the high cost of legal consultation. Traditional AI models attempt to solve this via
literal translation, which often fails to capture the true legal intent, while generic Large
Language Models (LLMs) are prone to hallucinating non-existent case laws.
This project proposes NyayaSahayak, an AI-powered vernacular legal document
simplification and risk assessment system designed for the common man. The system
utilizes a hybrid AI architecture. First, a locally fine-tuned BERT-based classification
model acts as a "Risk Scorecard," analyzing extracted contract clauses to flag exploitative
or non-standard terms (High, Medium, Safe). Second, it employs Retrieval-Augmented
Generation (RAG) integrated with a vector database (ChromaDB) of verified Indian case
laws to ensure factual, zero-hallucination precedent recommendations. Finally, a cloud-
based generative LLM simplifies the legal intent and translates the insights into regional
languages, specifically Marathi and Hindi. By automating compliance checks and
bridging the language gap, NyayaSahayak democratizes access to legal intelligence,
transforming complex contracts into actionable, easily understandable insights for non-
technical users.

<b>Application Gaps and Objectives of the Proposed Project</b>
<b>Application Gaps:</b>
Gap 1: B2B Focus: Existing LegalTech tools (SpotDraft, Jhana) are built for lawyers and
enterprises. There is no B2C tool that helps a common citizen understand an everyday contract
(like a rent agreement) before signing it.
Gap 2: The Hallucination Trap: General generative AIs (ChatGPT, Gemini) frequently invent
fake court cases and citations when asked legal questions, making them dangerous for legal
analysis.
Gap 3: Literal vs. Legal Translation: Tools like Google Translate provide literal word-for-
word translations, which fail to capture the actual legal intent or consequence of a clause in
regional languages like Marathi.
Gap 4: Lack of Actionable Risk Detection: Current summarizers just shorten the text; they do
not proactively warn the user if a specific clause is illegal or highly exploitative compared to
state laws.
<b>Objectives:</b>
<b>Objective 1:</b> To develop a user-friendly, B2C web application that accepts standard legal PDFs
and explains them in simple terms, empowering non-experts.
Objective 2: To implement a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) pipeline using
ChromaDB to ensure the AI only answers based on verified, uploaded statutes and Kanoon
judgments.
Objective 3: To utilize context-aware prompt engineering to translate the legal consequence
(ELI5 - Explain Like I'm 5) into Marathi/Hindi, rather than just translating the vocabulary.
Objective 4: To train a custom local classification model (BERT-based) that acts as a "Risk
Scorecard," automatically flagging clauses as Safe (Green), Medium Risk (Yellow), or High
Risk (Red).

Frontend & User Interface
Built as a responsive web application (using React.js or Streamlit) designed for high
<math>\bullet</math>
accessibility and ease of use for non-technical users.
The UI will feature a multi-lingual interface (specifically English, Marathi, and Hindi)
<math>\bullet</math>
to cater to the regional demographic.
Includes a dynamic "Risk Scorecard" dashboard and a dual-panel view: one side
•
displaying the original uploaded PDF and the other showing the simplified vernacular
summary.
<b>Document Ingestion & Parsing Engine</b>
Integration of PyMuPDF for high-speed, layout-aware text extraction from digital PDF
•
contracts to maintain structural integrity.
Implementation of Tesseract OCR configured with Indic language packs to accurately
•
digitize scanned physical documents, stamped agreements, and court notices.
A semantic chunking algorithm will split the extracted text logically by legal clauses
•
rather than arbitrary word counts, preserving the complete legal context of every
sentence.
<b>Custom AI Model (Clause Risk Classifier)</b>
A locally fine-tuned, lightweight NLP model (based on DistilBERT or InLegalBERT)
<math>\bullet</math>
will be trained on a custom dataset of standard vs. exploitative legal clauses.
This discriminative model will analyse every extracted clause and classify it into three
•
risk tiers: Safe (Green), Medium Risk (Yellow), or High Risk (Red).
This fulfills the academic requirement of custom model training while maintaining low
•
hardware resource consumption.
<b>Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) Pipeline</b>
A local vector database (ChromaDB) will be established and populated with a curated
•
dataset of landmark Indian court judgments (e.g., from Indian Kanoon).
The system will convert the user's specific legal issue into vector embeddings using
•
HuggingFace models (all-MiniLM-L6-v2).
It will mathematically retrieve the top 3 most similar past cases, grounding all precedent
•
suggestions in absolute legal fact and ensuring zero AI hallucination.

<b>Generative Summarization & Translation (LLM Integration)</b>
Secure backend integration with an advanced generative API (such as Groq/Llama-3 or
<math>\bullet</math>
Gemini) to handle complex reasoning and translation tasks.
Utilizing strict "Chain-of-Thought" prompt engineering, the LLM will process only the
<math>\bullet</math>
High-Risk clauses identified by the local classifier.
The API will translate the complex legal intent, not just literal words into "Explain Like
<math>\bullet</math>
I'm 5" (ELI5) bullet points in the user's selected regional language.
<b>Backend Architecture & Database</b>
A robust backend utilizing Python (FastAPI or Flask) will orchestrate the data flow
<math>\bullet</math>
between the OCR, the custom local model, the Vector DB, and the external LLM API.
PostgreSQL will be utilized for secure user authentication, session management, and
<math>\bullet</math>
storing encrypted audit logs of analysed documents.

<b>Expected Outcomes</b>
1. <b>Functional B2C Prototype:</b> A fully operational web application (via Streamlit/FastAPI)
where users can upload complex legal PDFs and receive analysis within seconds.
2. High-Accuracy Risk Detection: The custom-trained BERT model will successfully classify
standard vs. exploitative clauses with over 85% accuracy, displaying them in an intuitive
visual scorecard.
3. <b>Zero-Hallucination Querying:</b> The integration of the ChromaDB RAG pipeline will ensure
that 100% of suggested precedent cases are real, verified judgments, completely eliminating
generative hallucinations.
4. Accurate Vernacular Simplification: Generation of simplified legal summaries in Marathi
and Hindi that explain the intent of the law, rather than providing confusing literal
translations.
Reduced Legal Review Time: The system will reduce the time taken for a layman to
5.
understand a 20-page agreement from several hours (or requiring paid legal counsel) to under
few minutes.
